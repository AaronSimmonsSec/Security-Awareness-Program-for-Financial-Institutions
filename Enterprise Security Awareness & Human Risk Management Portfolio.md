## **Executive Summary**
This portfolio presents a complete enterprise Security Awareness and Human Risk Management framework aligned to the Cyber Risk Institute (CRI) Profile and designed specifically for a highly regulated financial institution environment.

The framework demonstrates:
- Enterprise governance alignment
- Human-risk reduction methodology
- Threat-informed awareness operations
- Regulatory defensibility
- Behavioral analytics and KPI measurement
- Executive reporting strategy
- Fraud-awareness integration
- Security culture maturity development
- Role-based awareness architecture
- Operational resilience alignment
- Board-level cyber risk communication

Aligned to:

## **1. Program Vision & Strategic Objectives**

### **Mission Statement**
Build and sustain a measurable, threat-informed Security Awareness and Human Risk Management Program that reduces human-enabled cyber, fraud, and operational risk while strengthening enterprise resilience.

### **Strategic Objectives**
- Reduce phishing susceptibility enterprise-wide
- Increase suspicious activity reporting rates
- Operationalize role-based education
- Align awareness to active threat conditions
- Improve executive engagement
- Strengthen fraud and social-engineering resilience
- Support audit and regulatory readiness
- Mature security culture across the enterprise
- Integrate awareness into operational resilience programs
- Establish measurable human-risk scoring

### **Guiding Principles**
1. Awareness must reduce measurable risk  
2. Human risk should be managed similarly to operational risk  
3. Security culture requires visible leadership participation  
4. Metrics should measure behavior change, not only completion rates  
5. Awareness should continuously adapt to the threat landscape  
6. Role-based education is more effective than generic training  
7. Executive reporting should be business-risk focused
