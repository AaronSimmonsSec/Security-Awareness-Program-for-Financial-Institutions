## **Comprehensive Inputs to Program Development & Strategy**

A mature Security Awareness & Human Risk Management Program is built on a wide set of inputs that ensure the strategy is threat-informed, risk-aligned, regulatory-compliant, and operationally relevant. These inputs shape the annual roadmap, simulation themes, training requirements, communications cadence, and executive reporting.

---

## **1. Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Inputs**
Financial institutions face highly targeted and rapidly evolving threats. CTI provides real-time insight into adversary tactics, fraud trends, and sector-specific risks.

**Key CTI Inputs:**
- FS-ISAC intelligence reports  
- CISA, FBI IC3, and Treasury advisories  
- Threat actor TTPs targeting financial institutions  
- AI-generated phishing and deepfake activity  
- Credential harvesting campaigns  
- Cloud collaboration exploitation  
- Banking trojans and malware trends  
- Insider threat indicators  
- Fraud patterns (BEC, vendor impersonation, wire fraud)  
- Sector-specific threat forecasts  

**CTI Influence on Program:**
- Determines monthly simulation themes  
- Shapes newsletters and webinars  
- Triggers targeted training  
- Informs executive reporting  
- Drives risk scoring adjustments  

---

## **2. Regulatory & Compliance Inputs**
Financial institutions operate under strict regulatory expectations that directly shape awareness program requirements.

**Key Regulatory Inputs:**
- FFIEC Cybersecurity Assessment Tool (CAT)  
- NIST CSF & NIST 800‑53 AT, IA, IR, PL, MP controls  
- Cyber Risk Institute (CRI) Profile  
- SEC Cyber Governance Expectations  
- PCI-DSS training requirements  
- SOX governance alignment  
- ISO 27001 Annex A controls  
- OCC, FDIC, Federal Reserve guidance  

**Regulatory Influence on Program:**
- Defines mandatory annual training  
- Establishes evidence and audit requirements  
- Shapes role-based training expectations  
- Drives executive and board reporting  
- Requires measurable KPIs and defensible governance  

---

## **3. Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) Inputs**
ERM provides the business context for human risk, ensuring alignment with enterprise priorities.

**Key ERM Inputs:**
- Top enterprise risks  
- Operational risk themes  
- Fraud risk assessments  
- Third-party risk findings  
- Business continuity and resilience requirements  
- Risk acceptance decisions  

**ERM Influence on Program:**
- Prioritizes high-risk roles and business units  
- Shapes targeted training and interventions  
- Aligns awareness to enterprise risk appetite  
- Ensures integration with resilience planning  

---

## **4. Behavioral & Human Risk Analytics**
Behavioral data is essential for identifying high-risk populations and tailoring interventions.

**Key Behavioral Inputs:**
- Phishing simulation performance  
- Reporting behavior  
- MFA fatigue susceptibility  
- Password hygiene indicators  
- Policy violations  
- Secure remote work compliance  
- Privileged access behavior  
- Prior incident involvement  

**Behavioral Influence on Program:**
- Determines targeted training  
- Shapes risk scoring  
- Identifies repeat offenders  
- Prioritizes business units for deeper engagement  

---

## **5. Fraud & Financial Crime Intelligence**
Fraud is a core risk vector for financial institutions and must be integrated into awareness strategy.

**Key Fraud Inputs:**
- Wire fraud trends  
- Vendor payment fraud  
- Synthetic identity fraud  
- Account takeover patterns  
- Social engineering fraud  
- Insider-enabled fraud  

**Fraud Influence on Program:**
- Shapes simulation themes  
- Drives specialized training for Finance, HR, and customer-facing teams  
- Informs escalation workflows  

---

## **6. Incident Response & SOC Inputs**
Real incidents provide the most actionable insights for awareness and training.

**Key IR/SOC Inputs:**
- Recent incidents and near misses  
- Escalation delays  
- Misconfigurations or access misuse  
- Social engineering attempts  
- Malware infections  
- Insider threat cases  

**IR/SOC Influence on Program:**
- Drives just-in-time training  
- Shapes escalation training  
- Informs tabletop scenarios  
- Identifies systemic gaps  

---

## **7. Technology & Architecture Inputs**
Technology changes introduce new risks that awareness must address.

**Key Technology Inputs:**
- Cloud adoption and misconfiguration trends  
- IAM and privileged access changes  
- Collaboration platform risks  
- New tools or security controls  
- Endpoint and mobile security posture  

**Technology Influence on Program:**
- Determines monthly technology webinars  
- Shapes role-based training for technical teams  
- Informs secure configuration awareness  

---

## **8. Organizational Culture & Change Inputs**
Security culture is a measurable component of human risk.

**Key Culture Inputs:**
- Employee sentiment surveys  
- Leadership participation  
- Business unit engagement  
- Communication effectiveness  
- Cultural resistance indicators  

**Culture Influence on Program:**
- Shapes messaging tone  
- Determines reinforcement strategies  
- Identifies areas requiring leadership involvement  

---

## **9. Audit & Assurance Inputs**
Audit findings provide direct evidence of gaps requiring awareness intervention.

**Key Audit Inputs:**
- Internal audit findings  
- External audit findings  
- Regulatory exam observations  
- Control deficiencies  
- Evidence gaps  

**Audit Influence on Program:**
- Drives mandatory training updates  
- Shapes evidence repository requirements  
- Informs KPI reporting  

---

## **10. Business Strategy & Operational Inputs**
Awareness must support the broader business mission.

**Key Business Inputs:**
- New product launches  
- Mergers & acquisitions  
- Organizational restructuring  
- Customer-facing changes  
- New markets or geographies  

**Business Influence on Program:**
- Determines new training requirements  
- Shapes communication campaigns  
- Identifies new risk exposures  

---

## **11. Third-Party & Supply Chain Inputs**
Vendors introduce significant human and operational risk.

**Key Third-Party Inputs:**
- Vendor risk assessments  
- Third-party incidents  
- Contractual training requirements  
- Access level reviews  

**Third-Party Influence on Program:**
- Shapes vendor awareness expectations  
- Determines training requirements for contractors  
- Informs access-based risk scoring  

---

## **12. Workforce Demographics & Role Architecture**
Understanding the workforce enables precise targeting.

**Key Workforce Inputs:**
- Role taxonomy  
- High-risk roles (Finance, HR, Privileged Access)  
- Geographic distribution  
- Remote vs. onsite workforce  
- Tenure and turnover trends  

**Workforce Influence on Program:**
- Shapes role-based curriculum  
- Determines targeted training cadence  
- Informs communication strategy
